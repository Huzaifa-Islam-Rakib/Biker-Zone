Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If the helmet shows signs of wear, such as faded colors, cracked foam, or degraded padding, or if it's been over 5-7 years since purchase, it's time for a replacement. Additionally, replace it after any significant impact, even if it looks fine.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspect the tires, brakes, and chain for any signs of wear or damage, and make sure they function properly. If everything seems in order, it should be safe to ride, but consider having a mechanic check it out if you're unsure.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet and use front and rear lights for visibility. Adhere to traffic laws, stay alert, and avoid distractions. Signal your turns, maintain a safe distance from vehicles and obstacles, and use bike lanes when available. Regularly check your bike’s condition and adapt to weather conditions.
